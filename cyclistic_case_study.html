Cyclistic Ride Share Analysis

Introduction

  • Purpose: To identify key differences between casual and member riders in order to generate actionable insights for strategic decision-making.
  • Data Sources: Cyclistic Historic Data made available by Motivate International, Inc.

Analysis

  • Trip Volume: Member riders exhibit significantly higher trip frequency compared to casual riders.
  • Bike Preference: A clear divergence in bike type preference exists, with casual riders favoring electric bikes (57%) and member riders preferring classic bikes (55%).
  • Ride Duration: Analysis of ride duration reveals that casual rides average 21 minutes and 18 seconds, while member rides average 13 minutes and 47 seconds.
  • Weekday vs. Weekend Usage (Members): Member rider trip frequency is higher during weekdays compared to weekends.
  • Weekday vs. Weekend Usage (Casuals): While casual riders also exhibit a tendency towards increased weekday usage, the difference between weekday and weekend trip frequency is less pronounced than that observed among member riders.

Recommendations

  1. Recommendation 1: Explore strategies to encourage casual electric bike users to become members, potentially through adjustments to electric bike availability for non-members.
  2. Recommendation 2: Recommend a comprehensive analysis of the current casual rider pricing structure, including the potential benefits of transitioning to a ride-length-based model.
  3. Recommendation 3: Enhance the value proposition for casual riders by introducing a lower-cost membership tier that aligns with their lower trip frequency.
